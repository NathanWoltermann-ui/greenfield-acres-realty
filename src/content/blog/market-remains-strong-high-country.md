*Submitted by the High Country Association of Realtors:*

Local real estate activity through the first half of 2019 continued to show signs of a strong sellers' market. **More than 1,000 homes were sold from January through June** by Realtors active in Alleghany, Ashe, Avery and Watauga counties — up **10 percent** compared to the first half of last year (995), and up **23 percent** compared to the same span in 2017 (888).

Overall sales volume also increased: **$329.7 million**, up 25 percent from 2018's $262.8 million and up 49 percent from 2017's $221.4 million. The median sales price for the year's midway point — at which half of all homes sold either above or below — was **$235,000**, up from $221,500 in the first six months of 2018 and $207,000 in 2017.

Inventory keeps arriving: as of July 12 there were approximately **1,810 active homes for sale** on the MLS worth $326.55 million, with almost 946 added since early May.

Meanwhile, financing got cheaper. Since appreciating 5 percent back in November, the average 30-year fixed interest rate dropped significantly — **3.75 percent as of July 11**, according to Freddie Mac, near its lowest since September 2017.

Around the counties:

- **Watauga** led the way — an average of 93 homes sold per month through the first half, totaling 555; Realtors averaged only 83 homes on the market at a time, selling 93 homes worth $32.3 million in June alone.
- **Ashe** — 195 homes sold in the first half, slightly ahead of last year's pace, with a median closing price up 4 percent ($189,500 vs. $197,800 this year).
- **Avery** — 38 homes sold in June for $12.3 million, a high for the year and the busiest month since November; median closing price $284,500, up significantly from $245,000 in 2018.
- **Alleghany** — Realtors sold 81 homes worth $17.3 million in the first six months of 2019; the median sold price was $173,000, and June's 16 homes were the most for that month in three years.
