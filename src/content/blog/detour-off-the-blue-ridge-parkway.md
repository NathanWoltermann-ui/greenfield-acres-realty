*By Melissa Reardon — WNC Magazine*

"What happens in Sparta gets home before you do," laughs Sparta resident Ginger Collins, citing what she calls the town's unofficial motto. The retired marketing professional and Atlanta transplant leads the Alleghany Writers, one of several creative groups contributing to the blossoming arts scene in WNC's far northern reaches. The closeness of the community (there are only 11,000 people in the county) set against a spectacularly scenic rural backdrop is exactly the allure of this under-the-radar mountain town.

The locals are as friendly as they come, and when you consider the number of outdoor pursuits, arts and music happenings, and the town's close proximity to the Blue Ridge Parkway, it makes for a worthwhile stopover for visitors seeking to explore beyond the beaten path.

## Eat & stay

If you're motoring the Parkway, take in **Mahogany Rock** (milepost 235), the actual center of the byway, before checking into **[Glade Valley B&B](http://www.gladevalley.com/)** in time to slip into a front-porch rocker with wine in hand. For dinner, **Horizon Bistro** is a good bet — rib eye, lasagna made with beef from nearby Chilly Water Farm, bourbon-glazed salmon, and a s'mores lava cake.

Other popular eateries include **Nikolas** (the area's only fine-dining restaurant), **Becca's Backwoods Bean**, **[Main Street Pizzeria & Taphouse](https://www.spartapizzeria.com/)** (36 draft beers), and **[Muddy Creek Cafe & Music Hall](https://www.muddycreekcafeandmusichall.com)**. Alleghany's first and only brewery, **[Laconia Ale Works](http://www.laconiaaleworks.com/)**, is in downtown Sparta with brats, trivia, and live music.

## Music everywhere

Alleghany County has no shortage of Appalachian musicians. **Music on Main** happens the first Friday evening of the month, June through September — free music at downtown restaurants, galleries, and shops, plus an outdoor street concert. And on Tuesdays and Saturdays, don't miss the **[Alleghany Jubilee](https://alleghanyjubilee.com/)** for a full-on dance party — flatfooting, clogging, and square dancing to a live old-time band in the former Spartan Theater.

## Outdoors

**[Stone Mountain State Park](https://www.ncparks.gov/stone-mountain-state-park)** is only 25 minutes south of downtown: camp, hike 18 miles of trails, rock-climb the 600-foot granite landmark, or fish more than 30 miles of designated trout waters. Nearby **[Doughton Park](https://www.blueridgeparkway.org/poi/doughton-park/)** presents 30 miles of trails, fly-fishing streams, and two preserved family homesteads. The **New River** — one of the world's oldest waterways — cuts through the north of the county, and **[Dusty Trails Outfitters](http://www.dustytrailsoutfitters.com)** can get you out on the water via kayak, canoe, or tube.

No matter which adventure you choose, Sparta is the sort of place that reveals its riches the longer you stick around.

*Planning a trip? The third weekend in July brings both the [Alleghany County Fiddlers Convention](http://www.alleghanyfiddlersconvention.com/) and the [NC Mountain Arts Adventure studio tour](http://www.ncmountainartsadventure.com/).*
