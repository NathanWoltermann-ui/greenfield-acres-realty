Have you ever dreamed of quitting your job and moving to a place where it's so cheap you barely need to work? Forbes profiled digital entrepreneur Laura Cabrera, who left a $2,000-a-month rent in downtown Chicago in search of somewhere beautiful, affordable, and — critically — wired with fast internet. Her theory:

> "Maybe we had it backwards before. What if the best places to launch a startup aren't in the most expensive cities? Perhaps it's in the cheapest places wired with the fastest internet."

Her research produced a list of **seven cheap places in the U.S. with high quality of living, good weather and fast internet** — and one of them is our own front porch:

## Sparta, North Carolina

> "Anyone who has always wanted to live in a town surrounded by mountains will want to look into this area. I mean, how many mountain towns are there with gigabit fiber-optic internet and a lower cost of living?"

At the time, Forbes cited a median listing price of $128,000 in Sparta and median gross rent of $600 — with **gigabit fiber through SkyBest**. The other six on the list: Lake of the Ozarks (MO), Emporia (KS), Conway (AR), Statesboro (GA), San Angelo (TX), and Chattanooga (TN).

Sparta was the only Blue Ridge mountain town to make the cut — the secret national media keeps rediscovering: real mountain living, city-grade internet, small-town prices. Prices have climbed since 2017, but the fundamentals haven't changed.
